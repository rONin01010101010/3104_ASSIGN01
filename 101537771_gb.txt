George Brown College is a public college of applied arts and technology located in Toronto, Ontario, Canada. Established in 1967, it was named after George Brown, a 19th-century Scottish-Canadian politician, newspaper publisher, and one of the Fathers of Confederation.

The college operates across multiple campuses throughout downtown Toronto, including Casa Loma, St. James, Waterfront, and the Daniels building. George Brown College offers a wide range of programs in fields such as business, community services, health sciences, hospitality, technology, and the arts.

Known for its strong industry connections and practical, hands-on approach to education, George Brown College serves over 30,000 full-time students and offers diplomas, advanced diplomas, degrees, and postgraduate certificates. The college is committed to providing students with real-world experience through co-op placements, internships, and partnerships with leading organizations.

With a diverse student body representing over 100 countries, George Brown College fosters an inclusive and multicultural learning environment that prepares graduates for success in the global workforce.
